Audio Assets

Doom 64 used 124 unique sounds, the first 33 of which were for the soundtrack. These sounds assets mainly originated from royalty-free audio archives, which were also used by films and other games. The remaining sounds were made by Aubrey Hodges, sampled from synthesizers, or sampled from the PS1 Doom soundtrack. Not all of their origins have been found, however the table below was created from the work by the PSX/Doom 64 High-Def SFX Project.

Below is a list of the Doom 64 sound assets with their numbering and naming corresponding to various engines and adaptations. The Sound Bank numbering corresponds to the asset order found in the sound banks that come with the Nintendo 64 (WDD), Doom 64 EX (SF2), and Doom 64 Remaster (DLS) versions of the game. Sounds after the 32nd sound (gameplay sounds) are not present in the Doom 64 Remaster sound bank, rather these are included in the WAD. On the Nintendo 64 engine, the gameplay sounds are referenced by a MIDI sequence file rather than directly to the sound bank. Thus in-engine their numbering differs from the sound bank. The reverse engineering project of Doom 64 (Doom 64 RE) enumerates the MIDI references by name and those are listed below. The adaptations of Doom 64 Retribution, GEC Master Edition, and Doom 64 CE use a sound bank for the music assets, however their gameplay sounds are in their PK3 archives under different naming.
